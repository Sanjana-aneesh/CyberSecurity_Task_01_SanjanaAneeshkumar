# Cyber Security Task 01: Personal Security Audit & Cyber Awareness Assessment

## Objective
This task focuses on assessing my own digital security practices and identifying potential risks — the foundational principle being that cybersecurity starts with securing yourself before securing systems and networks.

## Folder Contents

### `01_Device_Security_Assessment.md`
Covers my personal laptop's security posture — OS edition and version, whether the system is fully updated, antivirus status (name, active state, last scan results), and firewall status across network profiles. Includes 4 supporting screenshots in the `screenshots/` folder.

### `02_Password_Policy.md`
Answers on what makes a password strong, why uniqueness matters, what MFA is, and the risks of password reuse — followed by a personal password policy with 8 best practices (length, complexity, no reuse, password manager usage, MFA, etc.).

### `03_Account_Security_Review.md`
A security check across 3 real accounts (Google, GitHub, Instagram), reviewing whether MFA is enabled, whether the password is strong, and whether a recovery email is configured — summarized in a table, with no actual passwords disclosed.

### `04_Threat_Research.md`
Research on 5 major cyber threats — Phishing, Malware, Ransomware, Social Engineering, and Data Breach. Each includes a definition in my own words, a real-world example, and prevention methods.

### `05_Awareness_Poster_Phishing.png`
A designed awareness poster on Phishing Awareness, covering common warning signs (urgent language, suspicious senders, unexpected links) and protective steps (verifying senders, enabling MFA, reporting suspicious emails).

### `06_Reflection_Report.md`
A 300-word reflection on risks identified during this audit (e.g., password reuse, missing MFA), the immediate improvements implemented, and why cybersecurity matters in general.

### `screenshots/`
Supporting screenshots for Part A: `os_version.png`, `update_status.png`, `antivirus.png`, `firewall.png`.

## Key Takeaways
- Reviewed device-level security settings (updates, antivirus, firewall)
- Audited password habits and built a personal password policy
- Checked MFA, password strength, and recovery settings across 3 real accounts
- Researched the most common categories of cyber threats and how to prevent them
- Designed an awareness poster to reinforce phishing prevention
- Reflected on personal risk areas and concrete next steps
