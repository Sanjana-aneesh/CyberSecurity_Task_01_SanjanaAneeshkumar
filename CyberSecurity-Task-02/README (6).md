# Cyber Security Task 02: CIA Triad & Risk Assessment

## Objective
This task focuses on understanding the core principles of cybersecurity through the CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability), and learning how organizations identify and manage security risks through real-world case analysis and a practical risk assessment exercise.

## Folder Contents

### `01_CIA_Triad_Research.md`
Definitions, importance, and real-world examples for Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability, plus a side-by-side comparison table.

### `02_Cyber_Attack_Case_Study.md`
A case study on the 2017 WannaCry Ransomware attack — what happened, which part of the CIA Triad was affected, the impact, and how it could have been prevented.

### `03_Risk_Assessment_Table.md`
A risk assessment exercise as a college Security Analyst, identifying 8 key assets, their threats, impact, and risk level.

### `04_Threat_Analysis.md`
Research on 5 major threat categories — Insider Threats, Malware, Phishing, Weak Passwords, and Unpatched Systems — covering description, impact, and prevention for each.

### `05_Security_Recommendations.md`
10 prioritized security recommendations with explanations of why each matters, based on the risk assessment and threat analysis.

### `06_Consultant_Report.md`
A one-page professional report responding to "How can we improve our cybersecurity posture?" — covering the top 5 risks, top 5 recommendations, and expected benefits.

## Key Takeaways
- Understood the CIA Triad as the foundation of all security decision-making
- Analyzed a real-world ransomware attack and connected it back to CIA Triad principles
- Practiced identifying and rating risk across an organization's key assets
- Researched common threat categories and practical prevention methods
- Synthesized findings into actionable, prioritized recommendations
