# Cybersecurity Posture Improvement Report

**Prepared for:** College Administration
**Prepared by:** Sanjana Aneeshkumar, Security Analyst (Intern)
**Subject:** How Can We Improve Our Cybersecurity Posture?

---

## Executive Summary

Following a risk assessment of the college's key digital assets — student records, examination data, the college website, faculty information, and network infrastructure — five priority risk areas have been identified, along with five corresponding recommendations to strengthen the institution's overall security posture.

## Top 5 Risks

1. **Data breaches affecting student and examination records** — the highest-impact risk given the sensitivity and volume of personal and academic data involved.
2. **Ransomware/malware infecting network infrastructure** — could cascade across multiple systems simultaneously and halt operations.
3. **Phishing attacks targeting staff and student credentials** — the most common entry point for larger attacks.
4. **Weak password practices and lack of MFA** — makes account takeover significantly easier for attackers.
5. **Unpatched systems and legacy software** — provides attackers with known, exploitable vulnerabilities.

## Top 5 Recommendations

1. **Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication** across all staff, faculty, and student accounts.
2. **Establish a routine patch management process** to ensure all systems are kept current with security updates.
3. **Implement regular, offline-tested backups** of all critical data, especially student and examination records.
4. **Conduct mandatory security awareness training** for staff and students, with periodic phishing simulations.
5. **Segment the network** so that a breach in one area (e.g., guest Wi-Fi) cannot easily spread to critical systems.

## Expected Benefits

- **Reduced likelihood of successful attacks**, particularly phishing and credential-based breaches, through MFA and training
- **Faster recovery with minimal data loss** in the event of ransomware or system failure, thanks to reliable backups
- **Lower risk of large-scale disruption**, as network segmentation limits how far any single incident can spread
- **Improved institutional trust** among students, parents, and faculty, knowing sensitive data is actively protected
- **Reduced long-term costs**, since proactive security investment is consistently cheaper than the financial and reputational cost of recovering from a breach

## Conclusion

Strengthening the college's cybersecurity posture doesn't require an enormous budget — it requires consistent, foundational practices: patching systems, enforcing MFA, training people, and preparing backups. Addressing these five risk areas would meaningfully reduce the college's overall exposure to the most common and damaging categories of cyber threats.
