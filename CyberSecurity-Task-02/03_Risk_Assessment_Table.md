# Part C: Risk Assessment Activity

**Scenario:** Acting as a Security Analyst for a college, I identified the following key assets and assessed the risks associated with each.

| Asset | Threat | Impact | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Student Records | Data Breach | High | High |
| Examination Data | Unauthorized Tampering | High | High |
| College Website | DDoS Attack / Defacement | Medium | Medium |
| Faculty Information | Phishing / Credential Theft | Medium | Medium |
| Network Infrastructure | Malware / Ransomware | High | High |
| Online Payment System (fees) | Financial Fraud / Data Theft | High | High |
| Email Systems | Phishing / Business Email Compromise | Medium | Medium |
| Library Digital Resources | Unauthorized Access | Low | Low |

## Notes on Assessment

- **Student Records & Examination Data** carry the highest risk since a breach or tampering directly affects student trust, legal compliance, and academic integrity.
- **Network Infrastructure** is rated high risk because if compromised, it can cascade into every other system (records, website, payment systems) being affected simultaneously.
- **Online Payment System** is high risk due to the direct financial and legal consequences of a breach involving payment data.
- **Library Digital Resources** is rated lower risk since the data involved is generally less sensitive and the impact of compromise is comparatively limited.

Risk Level was determined by combining the **likelihood** of the threat occurring with the **severity of impact** if it did occur (Risk = Likelihood × Impact).
